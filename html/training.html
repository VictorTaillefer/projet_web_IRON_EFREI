Train Smart, Race Strong

Training for a triathlon is more than just logging hours in the pool, on the bike, or on your feet. It's a structured commitment that balances intensity with rest, pushing limits while listening to your body.

Time Commitment: Depending on your chosen distance, expect to dedicate 6 to 15+ hours per week. Sprint races are more beginner-friendly, while Ironman distances require near-daily sessions.

Nutrition: Fuel is crucial. You’ll need balanced meals rich in complex carbs, lean protein, and hydration strategies that support long sessions and recovery. Pre- and post-workout nutrition can make or break progress.

Recovery: Don’t skip it. Sleep, stretching, and light mobility work are essential to prevent injury and support long-term performance gains.

Mindset: Triathlon is as much mental as physical. Goal setting, tracking progress, and pushing through fatigue are skills developed over time.

Gear Essentials: A quality bike, proper footwear, a comfortable wetsuit, and basic tracking tools can streamline your training process and enhance safety.

  • Train with intention, not just volume.
  • Log your sessions and listen to your body.
  • Consistency > perfection — show up regularly.
  • Adapt your schedule around your life, not vice versa.
